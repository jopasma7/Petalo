## 1. Introduction

Pétalo is a desktop application designed specifically for managing flower shops. From your product catalog to order tracking, events, clients, and suppliers — all in one program that works completely offline.

### What can you do with Pétalo?

- Manage your product catalog with real-time stock control
- Register and consult clients with their purchase history and preferences
- Create and track orders from start to finish
- Plan special events such as weddings, baptisms, or funerals
- Control inventory, receive low-stock alerts, and manage suppliers
- Generate visual reports on sales, orders, and performance
- Export data in PDF, Excel, or CSV format
- Process quick sales from the built-in POS terminal

### Offline operation

Pétalo stores all data directly on your computer using a local database. No internet connection is required to work. A connection is only needed when activating your license.

## 2. Installation

### System Requirements

| Requirement | Minimum |
|-------------|---------|
| Operating system | Windows 10 (64-bit) or later |
| RAM | 256 MB |
| Disk space | 200 MB free |
| Internet connection | Only for license activation |

### Installation Steps

1. Download `Petalo.Setup.1.0.0.exe` from the official website or the download link you received.
2. Double-click the installer.
3. If Windows displays a security warning, click **"More info"** and then **"Run anyway"**. This is normal for applications that are not distributed through the Microsoft Store.
4. Follow the installer steps. Pétalo will be installed automatically.
5. A shortcut will be created on your desktop and in the Start menu.

## 4. First Launch & Setup

The first time you open Pétalo, a **welcome wizard** will guide you through the initial setup:

1. **Language selection** — Choose between Spanish and English. You can change this at any time from Settings.
2. **Business details** — Enter your flower shop's name, address, and phone number. This information will appear on the PDF documents Pétalo generates.
3. **Sample data** — The wizard can load demo data so you can explore the application without having to enter anything from scratch. You can delete it whenever you like.

Once the wizard is complete, you will land on the **main Dashboard**.

## 5. Dashboard — Overview

The Dashboard is Pétalo's home screen. It gives you an instant snapshot of your business.

### KPI Cards

At the top, you will find four key indicators:

| Indicator | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Pending orders** | Orders that have not yet been delivered |
| **Monthly sales** | Total revenue billed in the current month |
| **Total clients** | Clients registered in the database |
| **Stock alerts** | Products below their minimum stock level |

You can click any card to go directly to the corresponding section.

### Today's Orders

Lists orders with delivery scheduled for today, including their status and assigned client.

### Quick Actions

Shortcut buttons for the most common operations: quick sale, new order, new client, view inventory, new event, and view reports.

### Dashboard Side Panel

- **Urgent alerts** — Products in critical stock situations that require immediate attention.
- **Upcoming events** — The nearest events (weddings, baptisms, etc.) so nothing catches you off guard.

### Sidebar Stats

At the bottom of the navigation sidebar you will find three figures updated in real time:

- **Sales today** — Revenue generated during the current day.
- **Low stock** — Number of products with insufficient stock.
- **Deliveries today** — Orders with delivery scheduled for today. Click to go directly to that list.

## 6. Products

The **Products** section is your complete flower shop catalog. This is where you manage everything you sell.

### Viewing the Catalog

Products are displayed in a table with columns for code, name, category, current stock, sale price, status, and actions.

You can **filter by category** using the dropdown at the top, or **search** by name or code using the search field.

### Adding a Product

1. Click **"New product"**.
2. Fill in the form:
   - **Image** — Optional. You can upload a photo of the product.
   - **Name** — A descriptive name for the product.
   - **Code** — Generated automatically. You do not need to fill this in.
   - **Category** — Groups products by type (flowers, plants, accessories, etc.). You can manage categories directly from the form.
   - **Purchase price** — What the product costs you.
   - **Sale price** — What the customer pays.
   - **Season** — Whether the product is seasonal or available year-round.
   - **Current stock** — Quantity currently available.
   - **Minimum stock** — When stock falls below this number, Pétalo will alert you.
   - **Description** — Additional notes about the product.
3. Click **"Save"**.

### Editing or Deleting a Product

The actions column in each row contains buttons to **edit** or **delete** the product. Deleted products are set to inactive rather than permanently removed, so you can recover them if needed.

### Managing Categories

From the product form, next to the "Category" field, you will find the **"Manage"** button. From there you can create, edit, and delete the categories used in your shop.

## 7. Clients

The **Clients** section centralises all information about the people and businesses that buy from your flower shop.

### Viewing Clients

Clients are displayed as visual cards showing their name, photo (if available), client type, and quick contact details.

You can search for any client by name using the search field at the top.

### Adding a Client

1. Click **"New client"**.
2. Fill in the form:

   **Personal details:**
   - Photo (optional)
   - Full name
   - Email
   - Phone
   - Address

   **Commercial details:**
   - **Client type** — For example: individual, business, wholesaler, etc. You can manage client types directly from the form.
   - **Usual budget** — Approximate amount this client typically spends. Useful for segmentation reports.
   - **Floral preferences** — Notes on what kind of flowers or arrangements they prefer.
   - **Important occasions** — Birthdays, anniversaries, or other dates you want to remember.
   - **Notes** — Any other relevant information.

3. Click **"Save"**.

### Client History

Clicking on a client card opens their **complete history**: all orders they have placed, with dates, amounts, and status. This is ideal for getting quick context before attending to a regular customer.

## 8. Orders

The **Orders** section manages everything your clients request, from the moment they place the order to the moment it is delivered.

### Status Tabs

Orders are organised into three tabs:

- **Pending** — Orders received that have not yet been delivered.
- **Approved** — Orders that have been delivered or completed.
- **Cancelled** — Orders that did not go ahead.

Each tab shows the count of orders in that status.

### Creating a New Order

1. Click **"New order"**.
2. Fill in the information:
   - **Client** — Select an existing client or create a new one on the spot.
   - **Delivery date** — When the order needs to be ready.
   - **Products** — Add the products included in the order with their quantity and price.
   - **Notes** — Special instructions, dedications, or any other detail about the order.
3. Save the order.

### Managing an Order

Each order in the list shows the client, delivery date, total amount, and status. You can open it to view the full detail, edit it, or change its status (from pending to approved, or cancel it).

> **Tip:** Today's orders are always highlighted on the Dashboard so you never miss a delivery.

## 9. Events

**Events** allow you to plan and manage special occasions that require advance preparation: weddings, baptisms, communions, funerals, graduations, and more.

### What are events for?

An event in Pétalo is more than just a date on a calendar. It allows you to:

- Plan stock needs in advance for that date.
- Apply special discounts to orders associated with the event.
- View upcoming events on the Dashboard so nothing slips through.
- Let the system account for expected demand when making inventory predictions.

### Creating an Event

1. Click **"New event"**.
2. Fill in the form:
   - **Event name** — For example: "Garcia-Lopez Wedding".
   - **Event type** — Wedding, baptism, funeral, etc. You can manage types directly from the form.
   - **Expected demand** — Low, medium, high, or extreme. Indicates how much work volume this event will generate.
   - **Start and end date** — The event period.
   - **Special discount** — Percentage discount applied to orders linked to this event.
   - **Preparation days** — How many days in advance preparation needs to start. The system will alert you accordingly.
   - **Description** — Additional notes about the event.
3. Click **"Save"**.

## 10. Inventory

**Inventory** is Pétalo's most comprehensive module. It is divided into six tabs covering all aspects of stock management.

### Tab: Inventory Dashboard

A general overview of inventory status with four KPIs:

- **Total products** in the catalog
- **Products with low stock**
- **Total inventory value** (current stock × purchase price)
- **Average product rotation**

Also includes two charts:
- **Rotation analysis** — Classifies products as fast-moving (green), slow-moving (yellow), or critically low stock (red).
- **No movement** — Lists products that have gone the longest without any sales or stock entries.

### Tab: Alerts

Shows all products that have fallen below their minimum stock threshold, organised by urgency level:

- **No stock** — Zero units remaining.
- **Critical** — Very few units left.
- **Low** — Below the configured minimum.

You can filter by level and automatically generate a purchase order for all products in alert status.

### Tab: Prediction

Pétalo analyses your sales history and stock movements to estimate how much stock you will need in the coming days. The prediction table shows, per product:

- Current stock
- Estimated demand
- Projected stock
- Recommended action (reorder, watch, no action)

The chart shows the expected demand trend over time.

### Tab: Suppliers

Register and manage your flower shop's suppliers. For each supplier you can store:

- Name and contact details
- Products they supply
- History of purchase orders placed

### Tab: Purchase Orders

Manage the purchases you make from your suppliers. Each order can be in one of these states:

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Pending** | Order created, not yet sent to the supplier |
| **Sent** | Order sent, awaiting receipt |
| **Received** | Goods received, stock updated automatically |
| **Cancelled** | Order cancelled |

You can create orders manually or generate them automatically from the Alerts tab.

### Tab: Movements

A complete log of all stock entries and exits. Available movement types:

- **Entry** — Stock received from a supplier.
- **Exit** — Stock consumed by a sale or order.
- **Adjustment** — Manual stock correction (for example, after a physical count).
- **Return** — Goods returned to a supplier or received back from a client.

You can filter movements by date and type, and register new movements manually when needed.

## 11. Reports

The **Reports** section provides an analytical view of your flower shop's performance.

### Analysis Period

At the top you can select the period to analyse: last 7 days, 30 days, 90 days, or 365 days.

### Main KPIs

- **Total sales** — Total revenue in the selected period.
- **Total orders** — Number of completed orders.
- **Active clients** — Clients who placed at least one order in the period.
- **Average ticket** — Average amount per order.

Each KPI includes a trend comparison against the previous period.

### Charts

- **Sales** — Daily or weekly evolution of billed revenue.
- **Order status** — Distribution of orders by status (pending, approved, cancelled).
- **Rotation by category** — Which product categories have the highest turnover.

### Rankings

- **Best-selling products** — Top products by sales volume.
- **Client segmentation** — Distribution by client type.
- **Top events** — Event types that generate the most work.

### Sales Detail

A complete table of every order in the period: date, number, client, products, total amount, status, and margin. You can search for any sale by client name or product.

## 12. Quick Sale (POS)

The **Quick Sale** is a simplified point-of-sale terminal designed for counter sales that do not require the full order workflow.

### How to use the Quick Sale

1. Click **"Quick sale"** in the sidebar or on the Dashboard.
2. Add the products being sold by searching by name or code.
3. Adjust quantities if needed.
4. Select the payment method.
5. Confirm the sale.

Stock is automatically deducted and the sale is recorded in your reports.

> **Shortcut:** You can also open the Quick Sale from the floating (+) button available on any screen.

## 13. Notifications

The **notification centre** collects all system alerts in one place. Access it by clicking the bell icon in the top bar.

### Types of Notifications

- Low or critical stock alerts
- Orders with delivery scheduled for today
- Upcoming events that require preparation
- Any other relevant system notice

### Managing Notifications

You can mark notifications as read individually or all at once. You can also clear read notifications to keep the centre tidy.

The **"Unread"** and **"All"** tabs let you filter quickly.

Access settings by clicking the gear icon in the top bar.

### Business Details

Your flower shop's name, address, and phone number. This information appears in the headers of PDF documents generated by Pétalo.

### Security

Change the application access password. You will need to enter your current password and the new password twice to confirm.

### Preferences

- **Currency** — Euro (€), Dollar ($), or Pound (£).
- **Date format** — DD/MM/YYYY, MM/DD/YYYY, or YYYY-MM-DD.

Shows the installed version, database type (SQLite), and support contact details.

From here you can also:
- **Export all data** — Generate a backup of all your information.
- **Clear cache** — Remove temporary files if the application behaves unexpectedly.

## 15. Data Export

Pétalo allows you to export information in three formats:

| Format | Best used for |
|--------|---------------|
| **CSV** | Compatible with any program (Excel, Google Sheets, etc.) |
| **Excel (.xlsx)** | Opens directly in Microsoft Excel with table formatting |
| **PDF** | Document ready to print or send by email |

### How to Export

1. Go to **Settings → Export data**, or use the export button available in some sections.
2. Select what data you want to export (products, clients, orders, etc.).
3. Choose the format.
4. Click **"Export"**. The file will be saved to your computer's downloads folder.

## 16. Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I need an internet connection to use Pétalo?**
No. Pétalo works completely offline. You only need a connection to activate the license the first time.

**Can I install Pétalo on more than one computer?**
The license activates Pétalo on a single device. If you need to move it to another computer, contact support to arrange the transfer.

**What if I lose my license key?**
Email us at bblottus@gmail.com from the address you used to purchase, and we will resend it.

**Can deleted records be recovered?**
Yes. Pétalo uses soft deletion: deleted records are marked as inactive but are not removed from the database. If you need to recover something, contact support.

**How do I back up my data?**
Go to **Settings → Export data** and export everything in Excel or CSV format. Save that file somewhere safe (cloud storage, external drive, etc.).

**How do I change the language?**
Go to **Settings → Preferences** and select the desired language. The change takes effect immediately — no restart required.

**Can I customise product categories, client types, and event types?**
Yes. From the form for each entity you will find the **"Manage"** button to add, edit, or delete the types you use in your business.

**Does Pétalo generate invoices?**
Pétalo currently generates PDF documents for orders and reports, but not official invoices with fiscal numbering. For legal invoicing, we recommend pairing it with a dedicated billing application.
